NASA monitors the SUN to understand SPACE WEATHER The Sun is a dynamic, ever-changing star. It releases massive amounts of ENERGY through events like SOLAR FLARES. These explosions can send particles into space. NOAA, in turn, monitors space weather conditions to help us prepare. When these particles reach EARTH, they can cause the beautiful AURORAS in the sky, also known as the Northern and Southern Lights.
